This time I wasn't captured randomly anywhere else like in my previous post. But it was quite some active last quarter with speaking, training and travel opportunities!

# Career Move to Xebia Microsoft Services
In February I had made a switch and started a new role at [Xebia](https://www.xebia.com/){:target="_blank"} Microsoft Services as a Technical Architect. A software architect guidance role with still some boots in the development trenches, just how I like it! During the initial conversations they also asked me what gives me extra energy and excitement at my job. For me it's being able to share my knowledge and experiences with others, and going on adventures while doing so. And they certainly heard me right!

There is already a default knowledge sharing culture and planning set up within the company, where we at least get together every 2 weeks to share and talk about specific topics or to just catch up with each other. And I've also been part of a regular team going to customers to provide `GitHub Copilot` trainings and hackathons.

# No more 'But it worked for me!': Dev Environments with Compose and Aspire
But the first external speaking opportunity which I was offered came up mid April for PXL Hasselt. A half a day dive into `Aspire` and how it originated and uses `Containers` and `Compose` with a class of students. I had also thrown in hosting your own LLM model with Ollama in Aspire and how to use it with `Microsoft.Extensions.AI` and `Microsoft Agent Framework`. Funny enough, the students had just seen `Containers` and `Compose` in their classes. So my talk became just an engine to start general conversations about the topics, and other things around them, and how we actually use them in the real production world. This felt just awesome to not be bound to a specific topic and be able to fill in those gaps with real world experiences and stories! Certainly something to do again, but immediately with that format in mind!
